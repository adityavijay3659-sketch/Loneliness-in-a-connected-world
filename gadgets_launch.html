The General Atomics MQ-9 Reaper (sometimes called Predator B) is a medium-altitude long-endurance unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV, one component of an unmanned aircraft system (UAS)) capable of remotely controlled or autonomous flight operations, developed by General Atomics Aeronautical Systems (GA-ASI) primarily for the United States Air Force (USAF). The MQ-9 and other UAVs are referred to as Remotely Piloted Vehicles/Aircraft (RPV/RPA) by the USAF to indicate ground control by humans.[2][3]


Features

The Reaper is part of a remotely piloted aircraft system. A fully operational system consists of sensor and weapon-equipped aircraft, ground control station, Predator Primary Satellite Link and spare equipment along with operations and maintenance crews for deployed 24-hour missions.

ARMAMENTS:

  • AGM-114 Hellfire (Hellfire II and variants) — primary air-to-ground, laser (or radar/GPS for some variants) guided missile used for precision strikes (anti-armor / point targets). MQ-9s typically carry Hellfires on wing pylons.
  • GBU-12 Paveway II — 500 lb class laser-guided Mk-82 bomb (laser seeker + Mk-82 warhead) used where a laser-designated precision strike is required. MQ-9 can employ these using its laser designator.
  • GBU-38 JDAM (and GBU-49 laser-JDAM) — 500 lb Mk-82 with GPS/INS guidance (GBU-38) or GPS + laser (GBU-49); gives all-weather standoff precision. The Reaper has been tested and fielded with JDAMs.

Versions:
  • Weaponized MALE UAV used for precision air-to-ground strikes.
  • Common weapons: AGM-114 Hellfire missiles and 500-lb guided bombs (GBU-12, GBU-38/49).
  • Seven external hardpoints; total external payload ≈ 3,000–3,750 lb (varies by config).

MQ-1 Predator vs MQ-9 Reaper - Upgrade Comparison

Is this is helpful in high combat firlds?

Yes, the MQ-9 Reaper is compatible for use in high-combat fields, but with certain limitations. It is primarily designed for intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance (ISR), and precision strike missions, making it extremely effective in modern warfare. With a high operational altitude of up to 50,000 feet and an endurance of 27 hours, it can monitor battlefields for long durations and carry out precision attacks using weapons like AGM-114 Hellfire missiles, GBU-12 Paveway II bombs, and GBU-38 JDAMs. This makes it highly suitable for missions such as counter-terrorism, close air support, and asymmetric warfare, especially in areas where enemy air defenses are weak or have been neutralized.
